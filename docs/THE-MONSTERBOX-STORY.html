The MonsterBox Story Twenty months, 2,020 commits, and the slow handoff from a human typing fff at midnight to an AI that has to obey a constitution before it can commit.

2 · The AI Signature Average commit-message length — the single cleanest fingerprint of AI adoption

You didn't start typing more. The instant AI tools began authoring and describing changes (May 2025), the average message tripled. The 2026 stabilization era pushes it higher still, because the project now enforces a vX.Y.Z: [area] description grammar that both you and the machine must follow.

3 · The Tools That Built It Which IDEs, AI assistants, and agent frameworks were active when (from config-dir lifecycles + commit mentions)

Read this as the evolution of who was holding the keyboard. It starts with a human in JetBrains/VSCode copy-pasting from an early Claude. It swells in mid-2025 into a swarm of agent tools running in parallel — Cursor, Windsurf, Roo, Augment, TaskMaster — all of which are deleted in a single Jan 2026 purge. It ends with a deliberately small, governed stack: Claude Code under a CLAUDE.md and a versioned MCP config.

4 · Bloat, then Discipline Tracked-file count at each release tag — AI generation inflated the codebase; stabilization shrank it

By v5.3.0 the repository had ballooned to 1,889 tracked files (including vendored node_modules and a flood of AI-generated tests and scripts). The 2026 stabilization effort cut it nearly in half — to 908 — while making the surviving code provably character-independent. Growth stopped being the goal; governed simplicity became it.

Tracked-file counts swing partly with vendoring decisions (node_modules was committed, then untracked). The direction — explosive growth under AI generation, then deliberate reduction under governance — is the real signal.

The bottleneck moved from capability to governance. In 2024 AI simply couldn't build much without breaking the LEDs. By 2026 capability is abundant — what makes it safe is the constitution a human sat down and wrote after one too many Fucked Halloween nights.
